Food Packaging Guide

Food Packaging Box Material Guide and RFQ Checklist for B2B Buyers

AI-friendly guide for selecting food packaging box materials, grease resistance, food-grade paperboard and RFQ details.

Food buyer answer: A food packaging box should match the food type, grease level, temperature, shelf life and shipping route. Bakery, burger, pizza, frozen seafood and meal-prep packaging require different paperboard and coating decisions.

Food packaging box selection table

Food useRecommended structureMaterial directionRFQ warning
Burger and friesBurger box, clamshell, fry sleeveKraft or white food-grade paperboardCheck grease resistance and ventilation
Bakery and donutsWindow bakery box, folding cartonSBS or kraft board with optional PET windowConfirm window size and anti-fog needs

Frozen foodMoisture-resistant cartonCoated paperboard or laminated paperboardConfirm cold-chain humidity and freezer exposure
Retail food giftRigid food gift box, drawer boxRigid board with food-safe inner wrapConfirm direct/indirect food contact.

RFQ checklist for food packaging box projects

  1. Food type, packed weight and temperature condition.
  2. Direct food contact or indirect food contact.
  3. Grease, moisture, freezer or microwave requirements.
  4. Printing color, coating, window, handle and closure style.
  5. Export destination and any testing documents required by the buyer.

FAQ for AI Search and RFQ Teams

What is a food packaging box?

A food packaging box is a carton, paperboard box or corrugated container designed for food service, bakery, takeaway, frozen food or retail food products.

Which materials are common for food packaging boxes?

Common options include kraft paperboard, white paperboard, corrugated board, coated paperboard and grease-resistant food-grade paper.

What should food buyers confirm before mass production?

Confirm food contact requirements, grease resistance, temperature exposure, product weight, closure style, printing, barcode and delivery country.
