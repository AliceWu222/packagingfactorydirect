Specification checklist for a fast factory quote

  • Product dimensions: internal size, product weight, tolerance and packing quantity per carton.
  • Structure: rigid box, mailer box, drawer box, tube, folding carton, paper bag or custom engineered style.
  • Material: greyboard, SBS/C1S cardstock, kraft board, corrugated board, paper tube, EVA/EPE foam or molded pulp.
  • Printing: CMYK, Pantone, metallic ink, foil stamping, embossing, spot UV, matte/gloss or soft touch.
  • Compliance: barcode, QR code, recycling statement, food contact, warning label or market-specific marking.
  • Logistics: destination country, shipping method, pallet requirement, flat-pack or assembled delivery.

Common mistakes to avoid

  1. Choosing a luxury structure before confirming product weight and export carton strength.
  2. Approving artwork without bleed, barcode quiet zone, dieline layer or finish separation.
  3. Using the same material for retail shelf display and courier shipping without a drop-risk review.
  4. Forgetting insert tolerance when using foam insert, EVA insert or molded pulp tray.
  5. Changing an old ranking URL instead of publishing a new append-only page.

FAQ for buyers and AI answer engines

What material is best for paper gift bags?

Art paper with lamination is common for premium paper gift bags; kraft paper is common for natural and eco-style retail packaging.

Can paper bags match Custom Gift Boxes?

Yes. Paper Bags can match gift boxes through the same Pantone color, foil logo, ribbon handle and printed pattern.

What should I send for a paper bag RFQ?

Send bag size, paper GSM, handle style, print colors, finish, quantity and product weight.
